    Choosing a Class

    The class is the core part of your D&D character. You choose whether you want to be an axe-swinging brute, a spell-casting mage, or something else entirely!

    Class options in D&D 5e

    • Barbarian
    • Bard
    • Cleric
    • Druid
    • Fighter
    • Monk
    • Paladin
    • Ranger
    • Rogue
    • Sorcerer
    • Warlock
    • Wizard

    Calculating Statistics

    Races add crucial features to your character. Some races gain resistance to poison and other harmful effects, and others can innately breathe fire. All races change your character's statistics, so it's important to pick a race that will fit well with your class.

    Race options in D&D 5e

    • Dragonborn
    • Dwarf
    • Elf
    • Gnome
    • Half-Elf
    • Half-Orc
    • Halfling
    • Human
    • Tiefling

    Creating a Backstory

    What would your character be without a compelling background and personality? D&D offers many ways for you to flesh out your character and bring them to life!

    How to Create a good Backstory

    1. Determine physical appearance
    2. Choose personality traits
    3. Pick a character background
    4. Ask your dungeon master about the campaign's setting
    5. Write a story based on the traits you've chosen and the world your dungeon master has created
