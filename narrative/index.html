    Digital Literacy Narrative v2

    Throughout this course and my journey with digital technologies, I learned a lot of valuable lessons. I underwent many difficulties and challenges, but I never felt frustrated by the lack of immediate success. Rather, I accepted these challenges and worked through them in a procedural way. My ability to use digital technology as a creative outlet expanded, but I also learned how to put my skills to other uses. After experimenting and practicing with HTML, WordPress, DITA, and Markdown, I grew much less intimidated of digital technology and found myself enjoying the work with digital technology instead of dreading it.

    Creativity and Digital Technology

    In my original Digital Literacy Narrative assignment, I elaborated about how I grew up using digital technology as a way to express creativity. With the tools I used and knowledge I gained, I can safely say that I have continued to express creativity with digital technology, and I plan to do this even more in the future. For my DITA assignment, I used a more “creative” Dungeons & Dragons tutorial as inspiration for my project. Taking this perspective on the project allowed me to see ways in which I could use DITA documentation for other creative purposes, such as setting up the background for a Dungeons & Dragons campaign. However, I also gained insight as to how I could use digital technology outside of a creative/artistic scope.

    Professional Uses

    Learning about digital technology and adapting to various forms of it helped me see a lot of areas where digital technology connects with technical communication. In this way, my digital literacy evolved into a more applicable form. The different types of documentation tools especially stood out to me as ways that I could organize information in a professional career working as a technical communicator. Larger projects might be performed with DITA, smaller ones with Markdown, and websites with HTML and CSS. I was also very excited to find out that the tools we use are also popular among the majority of technical communicators, which makes me feel accomplished in learning them.

    While I did not master any of the tools we learned, I was able to adapt to them quickly and effectively in a way that allowed me to efficiently complete useful tasks. In a sense, I think that this is more useful to me than mastering HTML and CSS. Since HTML and CSS only make up one part of technical communication, I would not want to spend all my time using those tools, nor would it help me. Instead, I’ve learned throughout the course that quickly adapting to new technologies is key for technical communicators because they frequently have to pick up new content and methods. Becoming a "Jack of all trades" ending up being much more beneficial to my digital literacy than becoming only a "master of one".

    What Comes Next?

    I’ve learned a lot of ways to apply my new digital technology skills, and I will use my lessons from this course to expand on and further apply these skills in the future. I think that these skills would make me a more competitive candidate in the technical communication field, and I no matter what job title I gain or what type of indsutry I enter, I will have to use them. Using strategies to adapt to new situations and projects is necessary for any job, so I am happy that I could build those strategies throughout this class. When I do enter the field, I may also have to learn new digital technologies outside of what we explored in class. In this case, I would use my methods and takeaways from this course to guide me through that process.

    In the nearer future, I can see a lot of things we learned in the class connecting to my courses in computer science and the rest of my TWC courses. Since I will have to learn different languages such as Java, Python, and C, I will use the new strategies from this class to guide those processes as well. Having the strong knowledge base from this course and other courses will also help me become a multilingual programmer. In Science, Medical, and Health Writing (which I am taking next semester), I may be faced with a lot of unfamiliar information. The strategies I developed here will be crucial in understanding this new information quickly and effectively, because I already know which information I only need to be familiar with and which information requires deep understanding. Thanks to the class, I have become much less intimidated by the digital technology landscape and have begun to actively seek out opportunities where I can learn more about the tech world. Whether this is other classes, internships, or extracurriculars, I have been branching out with my goals and my personal learning network. Because of this class, my digital literacy evolved in a fundamental way, and I am excited to see how my relationship with digital technology will grow in the future.
