    Course Reflection

    In this course, I was able to learn a lot of things about myself and about digital technology. Almost every aspect of the course helped me in some way, and looking back, I saw myself grow quite a bit this semester. By reflecting on my relationship to digital technology and then expanding on my knowledge, I feel much more prepared to use my existing knowledge and gain new knowledge about digital technology in a future setting, whether that’s school or career based. Ultimately, this was one of the most useful classes for my education in technical communication.

    Strategies

    I developed many new strategies for developing my skills with digital technology and technical communication as a whole. Specifically, I learned that gaining a base knowledge of the technology and then testing and playing with the technology is really beneficial to my learning. To summarize and illustrate my strategy for learning new digital technologies, I have created a sort of “algorithm” that I use.

    1. Set up the new technology – install, create environment, etc.
    2. Gain a basic understanding of how to use the technology.
    3. Test some elements and features.
    4. Come up with something that I want to create.
    5. Try to create that thing using the technology.
    6. Repeat when I have created something and want to learn more.

    I feel like this algorithm helped me learn new digital technology because I was always motivated to explore new things that relate to me, and so I was always passionate about reaching my exploration-based goals. I also found that unlike CMS technologies, the different tools we used in class had few limitations – the challenge came in learning how to use the technology to achieve your goal. Using a testing and tinkering period and switching over to a goal or objective really helped me explore many aspects of the technology we used and how I could use them to my advantage. I’ve also expanded this algorithm beyond this class; for other technologies like programming languages, I have used a similar method to learn new things. Without this class, I would not have learned how to create such a helpful method for myself, so I am very happy with how my learning strategy developed this semester.

    Key Takeaways

    I feel like my ability to grasp complicated concepts grew a lot this semester, to the point where I have been able to do so very quickly. In a technical communication setting, this is extremely important because we are often met with new, complex information that we have to work with. From a personal standpoint, this is also valuable. I now have a lot more artistic tools when I engage in my hobbies, and I also have a stronger ability to learn about new tools and explore. I like that I have broadened my horizons for both my schoolwork/career capabilities and my creative hobbies because I feel that a new dimension of the world has opened up for me when I previously didn’t know much about it. I elaborated a bit about this in my digital literacy narrative, but this semester I had the goal of learning more about technology, specifically programming and computers. What I achieved far exceeds this goal, as I have learned about these things and applied them to my interests, but now I am excited to learn even more. To me this is the biggest victory of the semester. I not only learned about digital technology, but I have also gained a strong knowledge base, a set of strategies, and a deep interest in the subject.
